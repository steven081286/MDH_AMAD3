Compound sentences

A compound sentence


  1. has at least two complete sentences, or independent clauses with ideas that are related.
  2. Independent clauses are joined by coordinating conjunctions, or a semicolon.
  3. Both independent clauses can stand on their own and still be understood.

Example

I am counting my calories.   independent clause

I really want dessert. independent clause

Compound sentence

I am counting my calories, but I really want dessert.
